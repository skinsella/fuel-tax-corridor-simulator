Irish Fuel Tax Corridor Simulator

An interactive model built on historical Irish data showing how a corridor-based fuel tax stabiliser would have softened pump-price spikes for drivers, and what it means for the trade-off with Exchequer revenue in policy decisions right now.

The consumer case is simple: when oil prices spike, the corridor trims excise to reduce the increase passed through to motorists. This tool uses real CSO pump prices, Revenue excise rates, carbon tax schedules, and Brent crude data from 2019 to 2025 to show how the rule would have behaved through the energy crisis and after it.

Why it matters

What the consumer feels

Lower spikes at the forecourt

The corridor does not eliminate higher fuel prices. It reduces the sharpest part of the increase when oil prices jump.

More predictable weekly costs

Lower volatility means drivers face fewer sudden jumps in the cost of commuting, school runs, and business travel.

Visible household cash impact

Translating cents per litre into a typical fill-up cost makes it easier to judge whether the policy is meaningful to households.

Trade-off is transparent

The simulator keeps the fiscal cost on screen so ministers can weigh consumer protection against foregone revenue.

Method

How the prototype calculates outcomes

Baseline pricing

Wholesale price is derived from actual CSO pump prices by backing out the excise, carbon tax, and VAT that applied each month. The no-intervention baseline then reconstructs the pump price at full excise rates.

Corridor rule

Excise falls when Brent exceeds the upper corridor bound and rises when it drops below the lower bound. Changes are bounded so duty stays between zero and 150% of the full rate.

Consumer welfare

Consumer saving equals pump price reduction multiplied by fuel volume, adjusted by the chosen elasticity setting.

Revenue effect

Fiscal impact combines excise, carbon tax and VAT changes relative to the baseline path.

Transparency

Data sources and assumptions

Pump prices

CSO monthly average retail prices for petrol (series CPM09) and diesel (CPM10), January 2019 to March 2025.

Brent crude

ICE Brent front-month contract monthly averages converted to euro at ECB reference EUR/USD exchange rates.

Tax rates

Revenue Commissioners mineral oil tax schedules including the March 2022 emergency excise cuts and phased restoration through 2023. Carbon tax per Finance Acts 2019-2024.

Consumption

SEAI National Energy Balance annual road-transport volumes for petrol and diesel, interpolated monthly.
